Poker Rules

Learn the fundamental rules of popular poker games like Texas Hold'em, Omaha, and Seven-Card Stud.

Texas Hold'em Basics

Each player is dealt two private cards ("hole cards"). Five community cards are dealt face up in three stages: a flop (three cards), a turn (one card), and a river (one card). Players have to make the best five-card poker hand from any combination of their two hole cards and the five community cards.

Betting occurs before the flop, after the flop, after the turn, and after the river.

Omaha Rules

Omaha is similar to Texas Hold'em, but each player is dealt four hole cards instead of two. A player must use exactly two of their hole cards and exactly three of the five community cards to make their best five-card hand.

Omaha is commonly played in Pot-Limit format (Pot-Limit Omaha or PLO), where the maximum bet is limited to the current size of the pot.

Seven-Card Stud Rules

In Seven-Card Stud, players are individually dealt seven cards throughout the hand, but only the best five-card hand using any combination of their seven cards is used to determine the winner. There are no community cards.

The game involves a series of betting rounds after certain cards are dealt face up or face down.
